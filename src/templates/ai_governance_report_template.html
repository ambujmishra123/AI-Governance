Remediation
Prioritised Action Plan
Top actions to close the highest-risk gaps, ordered by regulatory urgency
  • 1
    Implement DLP controls blocking PII upload to external AI tools
    Configure endpoint DLP (Microsoft Purview or equivalent) to block paste/upload of fields matching Aadhaar, PAN, phone number patterns to known AI tool domains. Apply to all corporate devices.
    Owner: IT / InfoSec Timeline: 30 days DPDP S.4 RBI IS Policy
  • 2
    Audit and renegotiate AI vendor contracts
    Review all SaaS vendor agreements for AI sub-processor disclosure and data training clauses. Addend contracts lacking explicit no-training provisions. Prioritise vendors processing customer financial data.
    Owner: Legal / Procurement Timeline: 45 days DPDP S.8 EU Art. 25
  • 3
    Create and publish an AI tool approved list with formal approval process
    Document all currently approved AI tools. Create a lightweight intake form for new tool requests (5 questions, GRC sign-off within 5 days). Communicate the list to all staff via email and intranet.
    Owner: GRC / IT Timeline: 21 days RBI Policy EU Art. 26
  • 4
    Draft and test an AI incident response runbook
    Define what constitutes an AI incident (data leak via prompt, biased decision, vendor breach). Set escalation paths, notification timelines (15-day EU AI Act window), and communication templates. Run a tabletop exercise.
    Owner: GRC / CISO Timeline: 60 days EU Art. 73 RBI Incident
  • 5
    Establish quarterly AI governance reporting to senior leadership
    Create a one-page AI risk dashboard (tool count, incidents, compliance score trend) for quarterly board pack. Designate an AI Governance Lead who owns the report and attends relevant risk committee meetings.
    Owner: GRC Lead / CRO Timeline: 90 days EU Art. 26 RBI Governance
